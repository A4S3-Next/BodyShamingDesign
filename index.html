Our Mission

Why this matters.

Bullying leaves invisible scars that last a lifetime. This campaign was built to put words back where they belong — as tools of empowerment, not weapons of harm. We believe every student deserves to walk into school feeling safe, seen, and valued.

🎯

Words Stick

Negative labels follow people for years. What we say to others shapes how we see the world. Words are not just words — they are the stories we carry.

🪞

Social Media vs Reality

Filtered feeds create impossible standards. Behind every perfect post is a real person with real struggles. You are enough — exactly as you are, without a filter.

🌱

More Than a Body

You are an artist, a friend, a leader, a thinker. Your identity is not defined by your appearance. No label can capture who you truly are.

Real Experiences

Words that were never just jokes.

1

“You’re too slow…”

During advisory games, a boy laughed and said to a girl, “You’re too slow, maybe we will lose because of you, you should exercise more.” It seemed like a joke to others, but it stayed with her the entire day. After that, she became self-conscious during activities and started avoiding games just to escape comments about her body.

2

“You’ve gained weight…”

At a family dinner, a relative casually said, “You’ve gained weight, be careful of your body.” Everyone moved on quickly, but she didn’t. That one comment made her self-conscious for weeks, and she began comparing herself constantly to others.

3

Mocked at the gym

A friend went to the gym to improve their health, but people started making fun of them for being overweight. They laughed and said, “Why are you even here?” This made them feel embarrassed and discouraged, and they avoided going back.
